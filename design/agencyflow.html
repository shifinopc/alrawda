Why this flow is different

The current system models three screens — Invoice, Receipt and Reports — which capture the money but not the journey. A modern Umrah/Hajj agency runs a longer lifecycle: an enquiry becomes a quotation, a confirmed booking, a documented pilgrim, a collected payment, an approved file, and then a fulfilment chain of hotel, transport, visa and ticket before departure — followed by post-travel close-out.

What changed in the real world (and why it matters)

Since mid-2025, Saudi Arabia’s Nusuk Masar platform sits at the centre of operations. A visa can no longer be issued on its own: the agency must first confirm a Ministry-approved hotel (which generates a Booking Reference Number, BRN) and pre-arrange transport, and only then submit the visa. The application flow must follow this sequence, track each step’s status, and store the documents each step produces.

Legacy flow (today)

Create Invoice → Create Receipt → (receipt used as the approval) → Print reports.

  • No enquiry / quotation stage
  • No pilgrim document (passport) capture
  • No hotel / transport / visa / ticket tracking
  • Approval is informal (via the receipt)
  • No departure manifest or post-travel close

Improved flow (proposed)

Enquiry → Quotation → Booking → KYC → Payment → Approval gate → Fulfilment (hotel→transport→visa→ticket) → Documents → Departure → Return.

  • Full lifecycle with clear status at every stage
  • Nusuk Masar hotel → BRN → transport → visa chain
  • Formal approval gate before fulfilment spend
  • Installment-friendly payments & balance control
  • Group manifest, vouchers, refunds, reconciliation
Design principle. Money and operations are linked but separate. Payment confirms commitment; approval authorises spend; fulfilment consumes supplier cost. Keeping these as distinct, tracked stages prevents the common problem of paying suppliers for a booking the customer never fully paid for.

1. End-to-End Booking Lifecycle

The master flow — every booking moves left to right

Sales / front desk Payment Approval gate Operations / Nusuk Travel & close

2. Enquiry → Quotation → Booking

Front desk / sales

Capturing the enquiry first means no walk-in is lost, quotations are tracked, and the booking inherits all the data already entered — instead of re-typing everything onto an invoice.

  1. Enquiry: capture customer name, contact, nationality and what they want (Umrah/Hajj, dates, group size). Source tracked (walk-in, phone, referral, online).
  2. Quotation: pick a package; the system prices it (rate × pax, add-ons) and produces a bilingual quote PDF the customer can take away.
  3. Convert: when accepted, the quote becomes a Booking (the equivalent of today’s invoice) — carrying customer, package, passengers, departure date and amounts forward with no re-entry. Status: Draft → Confirmed

3. Pilgrim KYC & Document Capture

Per passenger — required before visa

Nusuk and visa submission need accurate passenger data and a passport scan. Capturing this early (with AI OCR) removes the biggest cause of visa rejections: wrong or missing details.

  • Capture per passenger: passport scan, photo, passport no & expiry, nationality, date of birth, visa type, relationship (for families).
  • AI OCR pre-fills the fields; staff confirm. System flags passports expiring within 6 months.
  • Documents stored against the passenger and reused for Nusuk, visa and the manifest.

4. Payment, Balance & Approval Gate

Receipt → approval — the control point

This is where the legacy “receipt = approval” idea is formalised. Payments are recorded as receipts (full or installment); a manager then approves the booking for fulfilment based on the payment position. Fulfilment (which costs the agency real supplier money) cannot start until the gate is passed.

  • Receipts record each payment (cash / bank / card) against the booking; the running balance updates automatically. Over-collection is blocked unless overridden and audited.
  • Installments: a plan with due dates can be set; the system sends reminders and shows what’s outstanding.
  • Approval gate: a manager approves the booking for fulfilment when the agreed payment threshold is met (e.g. deposit % or full). Rejected/under-paid bookings sit On hold and cannot consume supplier cost.
This single change protects cash flow: the agency never books a non-refundable hotel or visa for a customer who has only paid a token amount, unless a manager explicitly authorises it.

5. Fulfilment via Nusuk Masar

Operations — the sequence is mandatory

After approval, the operations team executes the supplier chain in the order Saudi rules now require: hotel first (which produces the BRN), transport, then the visa, and the flight. Each step is a tracked task with its own status and stored confirmation.

Why hotel before visa. Since June 2025 the visa cannot be submitted without a confirmed, Ministry-approved hotel (the BRN) and pre-arranged transport. The system enforces this order so staff can’t submit a visa that will be rejected.

6. Documents, Pre-Departure & Return

Issue, manifest, travel, close
  • Documents: the dynamic print engine issues the bilingual visa slip, hotel voucher, transport voucher, e-ticket and a full itinerary — each pulled from the data already captured.
  • Manifest: a passenger/group list by departure date (the modern version of today’s Passengers List report) for airport and ground handling.
  • Reminders: automated pre-departure messages (documents to carry, reporting time).
  • Return & close: mark the booking travelled, capture feedback, archive the file. Status: Completed

8.2 Back-office & supplier payments

  • Supplier cost is captured per booking (hotel, visa, transport, ticket), so profit per booking = received − supplier cost − allocated expense is always known.
  • Office expenses and refunds use the same payment voucher as today, but categorised for analysis.
  • Reports keep the six familiar outputs (Income Summary, Pending, Income Detail, Passengers List, Departure-wise, Expense) and add a live dashboard: today’s collection, outstanding balances, upcoming departures, visa-pending count, and margin.
All six legacy reports remain — nothing the agency relies on is lost. The flow simply adds the missing operational stages around them and gives every booking a clear, trackable status.
